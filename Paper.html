Special Paper

Yiliang county in Southwest China's Yunnan province has kept a traditional papermaking handicraft with the raw material of tree bark. Producing a traditional handmade paper includes 72 individual processes and takes more than ten days.

The traditional handmade paper craftsmanship has been affected by modern papermaking machine technology and an increasing number of young people have been forced to leave the county to find work in other cities. Dai and Yang both feel that more awareness is needed to protect handmade paper craftsmanship.
